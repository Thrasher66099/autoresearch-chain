# What This Is and What This Is Not

## Purpose

This document exists to prevent confusion.

AutoResearch Chain is unusual enough that many readers will try to fit it into a familiar category:
- blockchain project,
- AI coin,
- benchmark platform,
- science DAO,
- mining protocol,
- decentralized training network,
- or open-source research tool.

It overlaps with some of those categories, but it is not reducible to any one of them.

---

## What This Is

AutoResearch Chain is:

### A fully decentralized protocol
The system is intended to be permissionless from genesis, with no trusted coordinator, no privileged validator class, and no founder-controlled scientific truth.

### A fork-native Proof-of-Useful-Work system
Participants use GPUs to mine validated improvements to AI training recipes rather than arbitrary hashes.

### A trustless adversarial market
The protocol is designed so that:
- proposers,
- validators,
- challengers,
- and competing branches

economically pressure-test each other.

### A research-discovery layer
The current protocol covers:
- autonomous agent-driven recipe search,
- block submission,
- replay-based validation,
- challenge-based falsification,
- fork competition,
- canonical frontier states,
- multi-domain research,
- cross-domain integration,
- and permissionless track creation through standardized genesis blocks.

### A market for AI training improvements
The current explicit target is mining improvements to:
- training recipes,
- code paths,
- subsystems,
- and related research domains.

### A foundation for a future training layer
The protocol is designed to later support long-horizon decentralized shared model training, but that layer is not yet fully specified.

---

## What This Is Not

AutoResearch Chain is **not**:

### Not a generic “AI blockchain”
The project is not trying to attach a token to AI because it sounds futuristic.

The claim is much narrower and stronger:
- autonomous research loops create a new useful-work primitive,
- blockchain can coordinate a trustless adversarial market around that primitive.

### Not a centralized benchmark platform
The system is not supposed to be a company-run service where a trusted operator decides:
- who can participate,
- what counts,
- what research problems are valid,
- or who gets paid.

New research tracks are instantiated permissionlessly through genesis blocks, not approved by a central committee. If centralized gatekeeping becomes required, the core thesis breaks.

### Not merely a science DAO
Governance exists in the design, but governance does not define scientific truth.

The protocol is not intended to be:
- committee-led,
- manually curated,
- or socially adjudicated.

### Not only a code-diff ledger
The chain is not just a history of patches.

It must expose:
- canonical frontier states,
- materialized code snapshots,
- pullable domain-specific codebases,
- and usable current working states.

### Not just a wrapper around `autoresearch`
Autoresearch-style autonomous agent loops are a critical enabling primitive, especially for Stage 1.

But AutoResearch Chain adds:
- a trustless market structure,
- replay-based validation,
- challenge games,
- fork-native competition,
- attribution,
- multi-domain research,
- and eventual training-layer coordination.

### Not yet a fully specified decentralized shared-training swarm
The current protocol does **not yet** fully define:
- long-horizon shared training,
- distributed checkpoint coordination,
- contribution proofs for continuous co-training,
- or the full persistent swarm execution layer.

That is future work.

### Not a guarantee of perfect truth
The protocol aims to produce:
- replayable,
- challenge-resistant,
- adversarially pressure-tested progress.

It does not claim to produce final, perfect, or fully objective truth.

### Not solved
This is still an emerging protocol design with open problems in:
- attribution,
- validation economics,
- scale transfer,
- collusion resistance,
- benchmark design,
- and future training-layer coordination.

## The Cleanest One-Sentence Description

If someone asks what AutoResearch Chain is, the best short answer is:

> AutoResearch Chain is a fully decentralized Proof-of-Useful-Work protocol for mining validated improvements to AI training recipes through autonomous research agents, adversarial replay, and fork-native competition.

---

## The Cleanest One-Sentence Description of What It Is Not

If someone asks what it is not, the best short answer is:

> It is not a generic AI coin, not a centralized benchmark platform, and not yet a fully specified decentralized long-horizon shared-training network.

---

## Why This Distinction Matters

If the project is underspecified, people will project the wrong category onto it.

If it is overclaimed, people will dismiss it as hype.

The project should be understood precisely:
- ambitious,
- adversarial,
- decentralized,
- research-oriented,
- still early,
- and explicitly staged.

That clarity matters.
